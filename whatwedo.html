WHAT WE DO

On education PWHE believes that with opportunity towards quality education for the pastoralist children, they can excel and compete among the larger community of Kenyan society. PWHE has supported vulnerable children from Primary to college level. Some of the supported orphans are now in meaningful gains. PWHE rescued a girl from early marriage; and with support from ActionAid, she completed her secondary school and currently teaching at Early Childhood development centre initiated by Pastoralist Women for Health and Education. PWHE through support of ActionAid was able to conduct budget tracking activities through trained social auditors and formed oversight committees in Merti district to monitor the development work on issues of water, health and education. Through girl child education campaign, PWHE has managed to train parents, teachers and pupils in primary school on the need to curb absenteeism by girls due to menstruation and later supported the use of sanitary pads as a coping mechanism. Through this training parents accepted to purchase sanitary pads for their primary school going girls, this has helped in retention of girls in school. PWHE collaborates with seven primary school peace clubs and two secondary in Isiolo County. PWHE is currently supporting over 1270 orphans and vulnerable children through APHIA Plus Imaarisha programme. PWHE has office land where it intends to construct its offices and save on rent in future.

CURRENT PROGRAMME Strengthening child protection system focusing on accelerating abandonment of female genital mutilation (FGM) in Marsabit county

PWHE has carried social audit with the use of trained social auditors and was able to form oversight committee that could oversee the development project in Merti. The targeted devolved structures were that of Constituency Development Fund (CDF) and Local Authority Transfer Fund (LATIF). There is high need of the advocacy to continue as advocacy proved to be a good platform for communities to engage their leaders and air their development priorities. Women Economic Empowerment- Seed capital of Kshs. 50,000, for twenty community groups was implemented through support from CordAid Netherlands. In addition, the group benefited from entrepreneurship training and education tour to Machakos County. Currently, PWHE is supporting with revolving funds for youth and women for its member groups towards alternative livelihood. Civic Education on the new constitution was implemented in fourteen villages in Isiolo County two months to general election. The number of participants trained ranged between 50 participants to 70 respectively. The area targeted included Isiolo Central, Garemara, G/Tulla District and Merti respectively. Towards the end of 2013 PWHE was supported by ActionAid with one of support on gender based violence on issues of female genital mutilation and radio session covering among others issues on poor performance in school, health issues and concerns and drug abuse among the youth in Isiolo County.

PWHE is supported by CordAid on Peace building and cohesion. The organization implemented over ten peace dialogue meeting for the warring pastoralist communities of Isiolo County in 2012-2013 with collaboration of Provincial administration and District Peace Committee. The effort has gained relative peace in Isiolo to date. On course with the programme of community peace dialogue meeting, training, school peace clubs support to alternative livelihood for pastoralist communities among others.
